Tamota Pappu

A traditional Indian meal includes Roti/Rice, Curry, Pappu/Dal, Rasam/Sambar and homemade yogurt! Pickles and Chutneys that are available in Indian Kitchens all through-out the year also make a frequent or regular appearance (based on the likes) on the plate and so do vadiyalu/papadams whenever we are feeling a little fancy.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

30 min.

serving

6 servings

Tags : beef breakfast pancakes food

instructions

step 1

Take toor dal in the pressure cooker. Rinse the dal well under running water. Add about 3 cups of water (double the amount of dal) to the dal.

step 2

Add the chopped onions, tomatoes and green chillies to the dal now. Also add spices \– turmeric powder, red chilli powder and salt. Give a nice mix. Cover with the cooker lid and whistle/weight and let the dal cook for 3 whistles (may require more or less whistles as per you cooker).When done, remove the cooker from the stove and let cool for at-least 5 to 10 mins until all the pressure leaves out of the cooker.

step 3

In the meanwhile, take a small pan for tempering, add about 2 tsps of oil. When hot enough, add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, garlic, dry red chillies and curry leaves. Let them splutter in the oil for about 2 to 3 minutes making sure they don\’t get charred.

step 3

When the cooker has cooled, remove the lid, add the tempering and give a nice mix. Add a cup of water or more if you like the dal to be of thin consistency.Adjust salt if needed and move into a serving bowl.

ingredients

1 1/2 cup Toor dals

Tamotoes, Onions, Green Chillies

Salt, Red chillies, Turmeric Powder

tools

Hand Blender

Large Heavy Pot With Lid

Measuring Spoons

Measuring Cups
